How Plastic Trash
Gets to the Ocean

Plastic is all around us – in our homes, our work, our schools and used every single day. Plastic is used to package food, , bag produce, make toys and many other very useful things. But, despite the helpfulness of plastic, it poses huge threats to our environment as well.
Ó

Plastic pollution in the environment is steadily on the rise in the United States. It takes approximately 500-1,00 years for plastic to photodegrade.

image
image

Unlike other trash, plastic doesn’t degrade with time, it photodegrades. Plastic photodegrades when it is exposed to the sun’s ultraviolet light.

Each year, enough plastic is polluted to circle the earth four times. A huge contributing factor to this is that 50 percent of the plastic we use is only used one time before it is thrown away

image
image

The average American throws away 4.4 pounds of plastic every day or 185 pounds a year. Only five percent of polluted plastic produced is recovered every year.

Most plastic that is polluted into the oceans starts out on land as litter on beaches, streets or sidewalks. Land based plastic makes up 49% of all marine plastic debris. Rain and wind then flushes that plastic into storm drains and creeks which then lead to the ocean.

image
image

After plastic enters the marine environment, they slowly photodegrade into smaller and smaller pieces. Plastic constitutes about 90% of all trash floating on the ocean’s surface. There is an estimated 46,000 pieces of plastic per square mile of the ocean.

Plastic pollution is having an increasingly negative effect on marine life. 44% of all seabird species, 22% of all cetaceans, all sea turtle species and a growing list of fish have been recorded with having plastic in, or around their bodies.

image
image

Virtually every single piece of plastic ever made still exists in some shape or form.

You can help reduce the amount of plastic that goes into the ocean in many ways. You can opt to use reusable water bottles, containers and bags instead of single serve plastics.

image
image

The biggest thing you can do to reduce plastic waste is to recycle. If you can, try to choose #1 (PETE) or #2 (HDPE) which are the most commonly recycled plastics.
